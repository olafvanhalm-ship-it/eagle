# Project Eagle — Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
## Role Proposal & Mandate (Phase-aware)
**Version 1.1 | Confidential**

---

## 1. Context & Strategic Rationale

Project Eagle is an AI-native regulatory reporting factory, purpose-built to serve Alternative Investment Fund Managers (AIFMs) in meeting their obligations under AIFMD Annex IV. The business model is built on recurring subscription revenue, high gross margins, and a scalable operational footprint — characteristics that, if managed well, should produce a cash-flow-positive business within two years of launch.

The CFO is the **financial architect and steward** of this model. The role exists to ensure that the financial structure of the business — its pricing, its cost base, its cash management and its reporting — is as disciplined and rigorous as the compliance outputs the product delivers to clients.

The mandate of this role is fourfold:

1. **Financial control** — maintain an accurate, timely and audit-ready picture of the company's finances
2. **Unit economics stewardship** — measure and report the metrics that determine long-term profitability
3. **Financial threshold ownership** — set and enforce the financial guardrails within which operational and commercial decisions are made
4. **Third line coordination** — coordinate external audit as the independent assurance layer of the governance model

---

## 2. Strategic Design Choice: Financial Rigour as a Trust Signal

Project Eagle serves regulated financial institutions. The company's clients — AIFMs — operate under strict governance regimes. A supplier with weak financial controls, opaque pricing or uncertain financial footing is a reputational and operational risk to them.

> **Financial rigour is not just internal discipline — it is a client trust signal.**

Audit-ready financial records, clear revenue recognition practices and transparent billing are part of the same trust framework as the compliance outputs the product delivers. The CFO role is therefore designed to build this credibility from day one — not to retrofit it at Series A.

## 4. Responsibilities (Phase-aware)

### 4.1 Financial Management, Reporting & Control

**Phase 1**
- Maintain accurate books and records (in coordination with external accountants)
- Monthly management accounts: P&L, balance sheet, cash position
- Ensure chart of accounts is structured to support unit economics reporting from the outset
- Manage regulatory financial filings and tax compliance (with external advisors)

**Phase 2**
- Full in-house financial reporting capability
- Quarterly board-level financial pack (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, KPIs)
- Formal financial close process with defined timelines
- Preparation for external audit (statutory accounts)

---

### 4.2 Budgeting, Forecasting & Cash Flow Management

**Phase 1**
- Maintain a rolling 12-month cash flow forecast (updated monthly)
- Track burn rate and runway — flag risks to CEO with at least 3 months' lead time
- Maintain a simple annual budget aligned with the operating plan
- Scenario modelling for key decisions: hiring, pricing, infrastructure investment

**Phase 2**
- Annual budgeting process with departmental input
- Rolling 18-month forecast updated quarterly
- Formal cash flow management policy
- Working capital optimisation (billing cycles, payment terms, receivables)

---

### 4.3 Billing, Invoicing & Revenue Recognition

**Phase 1**
- Design billing processes aligned with subscription contract terms
- Issue invoices accurately and on time; track receivables
- Implement revenue recognition policy consistent with applicable accounting standards (IFRS 15 / Dutch GAAP as applicable)
- Ensure billing system can accommodate per-AIF, per-NCA or volume-based pricing structures

**Phase 2**
- Automated billing and invoicing workflow integrated with the platform
- Deferred revenue tracking and reconciliation
- Revenue recognition reviewed annually with external accountants

---

### 4.4 Unit Economics: Measurement, Reporting & Financial Thresholds

The CFO **measures and reports** unit economics, and **sets the financial thresholds** within which the COO and CRO operate. The COO owns operational cost drivers; the CFO owns the financial view of those drivers.

| Metric | Who drives it | CFO's role |
|---|---|---|
| Automation ratio | COO | Measure, report, and set the margin improvement target it must achieve |
| Cost-per-submission | COO (operational inputs) | Measure total cost, report trend, set the financial ceiling per unit |
| Gross margin per client | CRO (pricing) + COO (delivery) | Report actuals vs. target; flag breaches to CEO |
| CAC / LTV / Payback | CEO + CRO | Model, track and report per cohort |
| Infrastructure cost per submission | CTO (architecture) + COO (usage) | Report as % of gross margin; flag threshold breaches |

**Phase 1**
- Define and track core unit economics from first client: CAC, LTV (estimated), gross margin, payback period
- Build the cost-per-submission model incorporating COO-provided operational inputs
- Set financial thresholds: minimum gross margin per client, pricing floor, maximum cost-per-submission
- Report unit economics monthly to CEO; flag deviations immediately

**Phase 2**
- Formal unit economics dashboard updated monthly
- LTV/CAC ratio tracked per cohort and per client segment
- Gross margin trend analysis with attribution (infrastructure, labour, third-party data)
- Target: >50% EBITDA margin at scale — track trajectory and deviation from plan

### 4.5 Financial Alignment: Pricing × Delivery × Outcomes

The CFO sits at the intersection of three functions:

| Function | Their interest | CFO's role |
|---|---|---|
| CRO | Close deals; price competitively | Set financial floor; ensure pricing covers cost of delivery with margin |
| COO | Efficient delivery; low cost-per-submission | Translate operational improvements into measurable margin impact |
| CEO | Growth and capital allocation | Provide the financial data needed for informed investment decisions |

**Financial constraints for the product roadmap:** The CTO/CPO owns the product roadmap. The CFO's role is to define the **financial constraints** within which that roadmap must operate — the investment envelope available per phase, the sequencing logic by ROI, and the financial case for or against a build vs. buy decision. The CFO does not approve individual features; they set the financial boundary and flag when proposed roadmap investments fall outside it.

**Phase 1**
- Provide financial floor to pricing discussions: below this unit cost, no deal is profitable
- Flag misalignment between contracted price and actual delivery cost
- Produce financial model for each material investment decision; present financial constraints to CTO/CPO as input to prioritisation

**Phase 2**
- Formal pricing committee participation with financial veto on structurally loss-making proposals
- Quarterly alignment review: pricing vs. delivery cost vs. margin target
- Strategic financial modelling for new product lines or market expansions — delivered as financial constraints for the CEO and CTO/CPO to decide on

---

### 4.6 Financial Risk Management & Liquidity Planning

**Phase 1**
- Maintain a minimum cash reserve policy (defined with CEO)
- Identify key financial risks: client concentration, currency exposure, data supplier cost variability
- Ensure the company is not operationally dependent on any single client for >30% of revenue without a mitigation plan

**Phase 2**
- Formal financial risk register updated quarterly
- FX policy if revenue or costs in multiple currencies
- Debt facility or credit line assessment (if appropriate at scale)
- Financial due diligence readiness for Series A

---

### 4.7 Client-Level Financial Risk

Each client represents a distinct financial risk profile. The CFO owns **financial risk per client**:

| Client Risk Type | Owner |
|---|---|
| Technical risk — data model mismatch, integration instability | CTO |
| Operational risk — SLA breach, pipeline failure | COO |
| Regulatory risk — incorrect submission, NCA error | Head of Compliance & Risk |
| **Financial risk — revenue concentration, pricing misalignment, payment default** | **CFO** |
| Commercial risk — contract misalignment, scope dispute | CRO |
| Relationship risk — dissatisfaction, low engagement, churn signal | Head of Customer Success |

**Phase 1**
- Track revenue concentration: flag any client representing >30% of ARR to CEO
- Monitor per-client gross margin: flag clients where delivery cost exceeds pricing floor
- Track receivables per client; escalate payment delays to CEO and CRO

**Phase 2**
- Formal client financial risk register updated quarterly
- Client financial tiering: standard vs. elevated-concentration clients requiring mitigation plan
- Contractual protections review (payment terms, termination clauses) in coordination with legal

---

### 4.8 External Audit Coordination — Third Line

The CFO coordinates the **third line of defence**: external audit and independent assurance.

| Line | Role |
|---|---|
| First line | CTO + COO (controls in operations and systems) |
| Second line | Head of Compliance & Risk (independent oversight) |
| **Third line** | **External audit — coordinated by CFO** |

**Phase 1**
- Ensure financial records and internal controls are audit-ready from launch
- Select external accountants and define the statutory audit scope
- Coordinate with Head of Compliance & Risk on SOC 2 / ISAE 3402 audit scheduling (Compliance owns the assurance framework; CFO coordinates the external engagement and cost)

**Phase 2**
- Lead statutory audit process
- Coordinate SOC 2 / ISAE 3402 Type II audit engagement (in partnership with Compliance)
- Integrate external audit findings into the financial control improvement cycle

---

### 4.9 Investor Relations & Fundraising Support

**Phase 1**
- Maintain financial records in a state that is always investor-ready
- Produce financial sections of pitch materials and data room (in coordination with CEO)
- Ensure cap table, share register and equity documentation are accurate and current

**Phase 2**
- Lead financial due diligence process for Series A
- Coordinate with legal and tax advisors on investment structure
- Post-investment financial reporting to investors (board pack cadence)

## 5. Structural Position & Three Lines of Defence

The CFO coordinates the third line and provides the financial measurement layer across all three:

| Line | Role | Function |
|---|---|---|
| First line | CTO + COO | Own and operate controls in technical systems and operational processes |
| Second line | Head of Compliance & Risk | Independent oversight, rule validation and regulatory framework |
| **Third line** | **External audit — coordinated by CFO** | Independent assurance — SOC 2 / ISAE 3402 certification |

```
CEO
 ├── CTO / CPO  →  system + product   [1st line: technical controls]
 ├── COO        →  factory operations  [1st line: operational controls]
 ├── CFO        →  financial control + threshold ownership + 3rd line coordination
 └── Head of Compliance & Risk  →  independent oversight  [2nd line]
                                      External Audit  [3rd line, CFO-coordinated]
```

**Phase 1 combination:** The founding COO also carries CFO responsibilities. This is explicitly time-limited.

**Split trigger:** Hire a full-time CFO (or transition from fractional to full-time) at approximately **€1M ARR** or at first institutional funding — whichever comes first.

**Fractional CFO option:** A fractional CFO (2–3 days per week) is appropriate in Phase 1, provided the founder-COO maintains day-to-day financial oversight and the fractional CFO owns the control framework and financial thresholds.

## 7. Guiding Principles

> **Principle 4 — Single source of truth:** One canonical data model; quality, lineage and ownership are explicit and controlled. The CFO derives all financial metrics — CAC, LTV, cost-per-submission, automation ratio, gross margin — from the canonical data model in the central database. Parallel spreadsheets or role-specific data extracts that diverge from the canonical record are a design violation: the CFO escalates these to the CTO and does not treat them as authoritative sources for financial reporting or threshold decisions.

> **Principle 3 — Standardised product with predictable output:** Standardisation is the basis of margin; the CFO tracks the financial cost of any deviation from the standard model and enforces the pricing floor that reflects it.

> **Principle 10 — Founder-independent by design:** All financial knowledge and processes are fully codified in systems, enabling independence within two years of launch. The CFO is responsible for ensuring financial processes are documented and not dependent on any individual.

> **Principle 12 — Ambitious growth and profitability:** Cash flow positive within two years, >€1M ARR and >50% EBITDA margin. This is the CFO's primary financial target — not just a corporate aspiration.

> **Principle 16 — Location-independent by design:** All financial processes — reporting, billing, forecasting, audit coordination — must be fully operable from any location. Financial records, dashboards and control documentation are maintained in systems accessible to authorised parties regardless of where they work.
